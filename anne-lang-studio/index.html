About the artist

Anne Lang graduated from Korea National University of Arts in 2009 with a BFA in Illustration. Born in Kyoto, Japan, she moved to Seoul when she was 10 with her family. Entirely self-taught, Anne's drawings resemble a lot of anime characters she grew up with her entire childhood. Packed with a haunting approach, her current works resemble ideas of feminism, empowerment and raw magnetism. Her compositions feature females often in lonely, sad yet beautiful settings.

Around her childhood, Anne started playing with colors and experimenting with different kind of styles. Being born in Japan, most of her work is centered around anime and dark colors, an interesting combination. This combination is said to be one of the strangest, something you can't find in any digital drawings nowadays. Anne loves working with clients, loves hearing their approach and ideas and thinks everyone can learn a few things just by listening and applying the good advice in everything they do, especially in art.

After graduating, Anne started working as a freelancer for a couple of small clients until she found her own style. Now she works with big companies, on different projects such as anime styling, book covers and gallery openings.
